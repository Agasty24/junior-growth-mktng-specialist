# Selection Methodology: Why These 10 Experts?

## Overview

This document explains the criteria I used to select the 10 expert practitioners featured in this research on B2B SaaS newsletter and email marketing.

The core principle: **Quality of sources matters more than quantity.** We prioritized 10 genuinely strong voices over 50 generic practitioners.

---

## Selection Criteria

### 1. They Actually Do the Work (Not Just Talk About It)

Each expert selected runs or has recently run a newsletter, email program, or growth function at a real B2B SaaS company. They are not consultants lecturing from the sidelines — they are practitioners with current, hands-on experience.

**Examples:**
- Dave Gerhardt built Exit Five from his personal LinkedIn following. He didn't just write about community — he created one with 5,700 paid members.
- Brendan Hufford runs Growth Sprints and achieves a 47% email open rate in practice, not theory.
- Joe Cunningham is actively consulting with B2B SaaS clients on email strategy and deliverability.
- Elena Verna was SVP Growth at Amplitude and Miro. She did not just advise on growth — she led it.

**Why this matters:** Theory is cheap. Practitioners who share what actually works deserve more credibility than agencies selling frameworks.

---

### 2. They Share Real Insights Publicly

We only selected experts who post regularly on LinkedIn, run newsletters, or speak at industry events. Their thinking is visible, not hidden behind a paywall or a sales call.

**Why this matters:** Public posts reveal how they actually think. If they're willing to share real insights for free, they're confident in their ideas. If they gatekeep everything behind paid courses, that's a signal they're selling hype, not sharing knowledge.

**Examples:**
- Dave Gerhardt posts multiple times per week on LinkedIn about B2B marketing strategy.
- Rosanna Campbell shares her 11 rules for LinkedIn writing publicly — tactics she uses with real clients.
- Joe Cunningham posts about email deliverability, copywriting, and HubSpot workflows openly.

---

### 3. They Cover the Full Spectrum of B2B Email/Newsletter Strategy

We did not select 10 people who all do the same thing. Instead, we chose experts across different specialties so that you get a 360-degree view:

**Newsletter Operators** (own large audiences):
- Dave Gerhardt (40k+ Exit Five newsletter subscribers)
- Brendan Hufford (Growth Sprints, 47% open rate)
- Elena Verna (Elena's Growth Scoop, 33k+ subscribers)
- Emily Kramer & Kathleen Estreich (MKT1 newsletter)

**Email/Lifecycle Specialists** (deep technical expertise):
- Joe Cunningham (email copywriting, deliverability, HubSpot)
- Alyssa Armstrong (lifecycle email, SMS, in-app messaging)

**Growth & GTM Leaders** (broader strategy context):
- Joe Kevens (market dynamics, buyer behavior)
- Jess Lytle (AI in GTM workflows, operations)

**Content & Positioning** (how email fits into brand narrative):
- Rosanna Campbell (B2B SaaS storytelling, email as narrative)

**Why this matters:** Email does not exist in a vacuum. It works alongside content, product strategy, brand, and growth operations. By selecting experts across these areas, you see how they all connect.

---

### 4. They Show Evidence of Results

We prioritized experts who share or reference actual outcomes, not just opinions.

**Examples:**
- Brendan Hufford: 47% open rate (verifiable metric from his own newsletter)
- Dave Gerhardt: Exit Five grew to 5,700 paid members and $3M+ revenue — built on his personal brand and newsletter
- Elena Verna: Led growth at multiple venture-backed companies (Amplitude, Miro, SurveyMonkey)
- Joe Cunningham: Client case studies and testimonials visible on his profile

**Why this matters:** Anyone can claim expertise. Results are harder to fake. If they share metrics or have a track record, they've earned credibility.

---

### 5. They Address B2B SaaS Specifically

We excluded generalist marketing influencers and e-commerce email experts. Every person selected has direct experience with B2B SaaS buying cycles, account-based thinking, or product-led growth.

**Why this matters:** B2B SaaS email is fundamentally different from D2C or e-commerce email. Long sales cycles, multiple stakeholders, higher contract values, and different psychology all require different strategies. Generic email advice does not apply.

---

## Who We Rejected (And Why)

**Generic content marketers:** Lots of followers, but no specific B2B SaaS expertise. We wanted depth, not breadth.

**Agency roundups:** Agencies that list "top 10 email platforms" are aggregating reviews, not sharing real strategy. We wanted practitioners, not aggregators.

**Academics or consultants with no current work:** Some experts have impressive backgrounds but are not actively running programs. We prioritized people doing the work right now.

**LinkedIn influencers who just repost:** High engagement but low original insight. We looked for people sharing their own ideas, not recycling others' content.

---

## How We Verified Credibility

For each expert, we verified:

1. **LinkedIn profile activity** — Are they currently posting about their work?
2. **Newsletter or public output** — Do they run a newsletter or publish regularly?
3. **Company/role alignment** — Are they in a role that actually does this work?
4. **References in industry** — Are they mentioned by other credible practitioners?
5. **Public posts on topic** — Did we find actual recent content about B2B SaaS email/newsletters?
